■ « Human is dead »
🇺🇸
🇫🇷
Saturday, April 18, 2026
It's strange to witness the end of an era.

Just as the weights and activation thresholds of neural networks are unintelligible to us as humans, code will eventually become incomprehensible as well. Just as machine instructions are low-level, source code will also be downgraded to that level. Of course, like geneticists who are still able to understand the functions of certain genes, seasoned coders will still be able to understand some parts of the code but it will be impossible for them to master its immensity.

Codebases will just become the evolution of neural networks, « black boxes » that react to stimuli whose expected outputs are only the result of tests and whose insides will be nothing but a distant mystery. This is in my opinion also the end of open source code, since there will no longer be much point in sharing code that no one can understand and that could be regenerated by a simple prompt. Security through obscurity will paradoxically become one of the best ways to protect oneself.

We should see this as the industrialization of programming (which already existed to some extent) where craftsmanship has no place in a world governed by capitalism and the race for productivity. However it is clear that the companies that own the models act just like drug dealers, luring consumers with minimal or even non-existent prices, making them dependent and then increasing prices exponentially and pushing them always towards maybe more « powerful » but especially more expensive models (while diluting the performance of their previous products to force the hand).

Probably some people are still reluctant or doubtful about this subject, and I think the questions raised are similar to those posed by the question of transhumanism. In truth it may already even be a form of intellectual transhumanism. In the near future, it will be humanly impossible to compete against people who make use of it without being a user yourself. The era of techno-feudalism is looming on the horizon, and the tokens we consume may just be a loan on our freedom...

This paradigm shift will force us to profoundly change our society if we want it to survive, because the entire tertiary sector is affected, but eventually when the secondary and primary sectors will also be impacted by a similar revolution in automation.

The advent of Artificial Intelligence may be in some ways closer to the Manhattan Project than it seems, its use could simply annihilate all the foundations of our society, now digitized. Bank accounts, health, identity, information, criminal record, etc. Some will see it as an opportunity to reshuffle the cards and change the rules of the game, but there is little chance that it will turn to their advantage since they do not actually possess this striking force, they are only renting a fragment of it.

Maybe it's a pessimistic, conspiratorial or even alarmist vision, but... In a world where:

  • We have never had so much leisure to show who we are, but yet so fake, staged and superficial
  • We have never been so wealthy but yet so poor
  • We have never had so much knowledge and information at our fingertips but yet so ignorant and misinformed
  • We have never had so much freedom of expression but yet so little diversity of opinions and censorship
  • We have never been so connected but yet so lonely
  • We have never had so many creative minds and tools to express them but yet so few original ideas and where every creation is just a repetition of what has already been done

When we will really have no control over what we are, what we have, what we know, what we think, what we love and what we create: « Human is dead »

Is it an end ? Maybe not.
After all « God is dead » too and yet we're still here, but will we be happy to live in this new world ?

■ The « Death of Humanity »
🇺🇸
🇫🇷
Thursday, March 13, 2025
I can't say if we are getting closer to transcendence or insanity.

The problem is definitely that we are attacking Maslow's pyramid in the wrong direction. The goal of advancing technologically was to fulfill our physiological needs, not to remove everything that gave meaning to our lives. If trying to simulate human intelligence and creativity was already questionable, introducing relationship simulacra to fulfill emotional needs in the current state of our society is simply an aberration.

The very idea that humanity will become entirely obsolete, surpassed in terms of capacity, creativity and usefulness, makes me deeply uncomfortable. Like the « dead internet », we will witness the advent of a « dead planet ».
